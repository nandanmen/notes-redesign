First we have to find the total sales at year 4. Since growth is 30% every year, we can simply do the following to get final sales:

$200,000 * (1 + 30%)4 - 1

This results in a fourth year sales value of:

$439,400

Subtracting all COGS and operating expenses we get the pre-tax income:

$439,400 x (1 - 30% - 45%) = $109,850

Finally multiply by 1 - tax rate to get net income:

$109,850 x (1 - 35%) = $71,402.5
