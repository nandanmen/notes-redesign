Financial Statement Analysis

Strategies

Firms can generally be categorized to one of two strategies — differentiator or low cost provider.

Differentiators are generally luxury manufacturers. They sell a low quantity of goods with high prices.

Low cost providers are sellers of cheaper goods. They have much higher sales volumes but each good has a lower profit margin.

Differentiating between them is simple. There is a general rule based on their financial ratios:

  • Differentiator — high net profit margin, low asset turnover
  • Low cost provider — low net profit margin, high asset turnover

Where asset turnover is defined as sales/assets and net profit margin is defined as net income/sales.

Potential Equity Investments

To choose potential investment options we have to look into the current financial status of the company. Essentially there are four measures:

  • Liquidity — ability for a company to pay off its short-term debts
  • Efficiency — how well a company uses its assets/liabilities
  • Profitability — the company's capacity to make a profit
  • Solvency — the company's net income compared to its debts

Let's try to predict the future cash flow from the previous example. We are given the following data:

First-year sales $100

Annual investment in fixed capital
(as % of sales)		 5%
Beginning noncash working capital $75
Beginning cash $10

We know from the previous example that sales is $120 and net income is $12.6. Thus we also know the following numbers:

  • Noncash working capital — $84
  • Investment in fixed capital — $6

Now we need to find the investment in working capital. We know that the required working capital is 70% of sales. Thus the previous working capital would be:

70% x 100$ = $70

Therefore the investment in working capital would be the new required working capital minus the previous working capital:

$84 - $70 = $14

Thus the change in cash would be:

$12.6 - $14 - $6 = -$7.4

Since beginning cash is $10, ending cash would be:

$10 - $7.4 = $2.6
